Capital

Brasilia is the capital of Brazil. The city is located high in the Brazilian highlands in the country's Central-West region . It was founded by President Juscelino Kubitschek on 21 April 1960 , to serve as the new national capital. Brasília is estimated to be Brazil's third-most populous city after São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro.

Rio de Janeiro

Rio de Janeiro is the second-most-populous city in Brazil. Rio de Janeiro is one of the most visited cities in the Southern Hemisphere and is known for its natural settings, carnival, samba, bossa nova, and balneario beaches. Rio de Janeiro was the host of the 2016 Summer Olympics and the 2016 Summer Paralympics.

Nature

The varied biodiversity that exists within Brazil’s borders has been called the greatest collection of plant and animal species found on the planet. Brazil is home to 60% of the Amazon Rainforest which hosts approximately one-tenth of all the species in the world.

Sun and beach

Brazil’s best beaches make the place a shore lover’s nirvana. Brazilians take their coastal assets seriously, and beach culture is a big part of the lifestyle.
